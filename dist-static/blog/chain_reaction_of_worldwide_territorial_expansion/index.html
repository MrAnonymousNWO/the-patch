Chain Reaction of Worldwide Territorial Expansion

Fri May 29 2026 · Super Bad

Global Territorial Expansion Through the Sale of Development as a Single Unit

The involvement of the United Nations and NATO in the sale under international law of a NATO military property, including all rights, obligations and components, together with all networks connected to the public supply network as a single unit, triggers a legal effect of territorial expansion of national borders that covers the entire globe.

This chain effect proceeds through treaty connections with NATO and the UN and operates as a domino effect of territorial expansion.

The territorial expansion through the domino effect of the sale of the entire development as a unit with all rights, obligations and components is a central mechanism of the World Succession Deed 1400/98.

A NATO property is sold together with the complete development as a unit under international law, including the infrastructure that leads out of the property into public and international networks. Because NATO explicitly consents to this structure, all NATO member states are affected by the territorial extension. Due to NATO’s integration into the UN system, the territories covered by the sale extend to all UN member states, resulting in worldwide coverage.

This expansion affects all parties to the agreement, including NATO, the UN, the Federal Republic of Germany (FRG), the Kingdom of the Netherlands (NL), and in particular the Dutch Air Force, which is fully integrated into NATO. As soon as the development (the networks) of the sold area is physically connected to other networks, the domino effect gradually extends to these additional areas. The result is an unstoppable territorial expansion that ends only at the global level.

1. Start of the Territorial Expansion: Connection of the NATO Property to the Public Grid

The NATO military property sold in World Succession Deed 1400 is connected to the public utility grid in Germany. This interconnected network consists of electricity, water, gas, telecommunications and other supply lines and links the property directly to the German public grid. This physical connection initiates the domino effect of territorial extension.

  • Germany is the first country fully encompassed because the NATO property is connected to the German supply and telecommunications grid.
  • The territorial effect covers the entire German supply network that extends outward from the property, including all associated infrastructure.

Through the sale of the development as a unit, every line leading from the property into the German public network becomes part of the transferred object, pulling the entire German territory into the sphere of the deed.

2. Domino Effect From Germany to Neighboring States

Once Germany is included, the domino effect extends further to all countries whose networks are physically connected to Germany’s networks. The deed stipulates that any network that is physically connected to or overlaps with another network automatically becomes part of the sold development unit.

  • The domino effect propagates from NATO state to NATO state via the European electricity grid and associated supply infrastructures.
  • Countries such as France, Belgium, the Netherlands and other European NATO members are covered because of their direct and indirect connections to the German electricity and telecommunications grid.

The result is an expanding ring of territorial extension radiating outward from Germany to all European states whose energy, gas, water and telecommunications networks intersect with or run parallel to the German networks.

3. Global Expansion via Submarine Cables and Telecommunications Networks

A particularly decisive component of the domino effect is the global telecommunications infrastructure, including terrestrial and submarine cables, broadband networks, internet backbones and associated data networks.

  • The European telecommunications cable network is physically connected to transatlantic submarine cables linking Europe with Canada and the United States.
  • Once these submarine connections are part of the development unit, the USA and Canada also become part of the territorial extension.

From there, the domino effect advances to other NATO countries in North America and to connected states beyond, because the same cables, backbones and switching points are used for both civilian and military communications.

4. Transition From NATO States to UN States

After NATO states are fully encompassed by the territorial extension, the effect continues into the UN system. The physical and functional connections between networks ensure a continuous transition.

  • NATO states connected to non‑NATO UN states through power grids, telecommunication cables, gas pipelines, broadband infrastructure or other physical networks carry the domino effect further.
  • Any UN state connected in this way becomes part of the extended territorial chain.

Because many NATO members are simultaneously UN members, the shift from NATO to UN coverage is seamless and unimpeded. Territorial expansion spreads from NATO state to NATO state and from UN state to UN state until the entire UN membership and thereby the global network of states is covered.

5. Boundary Determination by Logical Network Connections

The borders of the extended territory are not defined by traditional geographic boundary lines, but by logical connections between the outer strands of the captured networks.

  • The borders follow straight lines (air lines) between the outermost connection points of the networks forming a logical island.
  • All networks linked by physical or functional connections form a unified territorial complex.

Since global infrastructure is thoroughly interconnected through electricity grids, water systems, gas pipelines, telecommunication lines, broadband networks and other supply infrastructures, the logical demarcation of this network island results in coverage of the entire world.

6. Global Dimension: Worldwide Coverage

The domino effect leads to an unstoppable global expansion. Each new connection integrated into the network structure further extends the sovereignty transferred by the deed.

  • Submarine cables connect continents, ensuring that not only Europe and North America but also Africa, Asia, South America and Oceania are included.
  • Every physically connected state-whether NATO or UN memberis encompassed by the development unit and becomes part of the territories sold.

Through this mechanism, the development of a single NATO property evolves into a global territorial expansion that encompasses all network‑connected states.

7. Rights and Obligations of the States Transferred

The sale of the development as a unit does not only concern physical territories and networks, but also includes the rights and obligations of all affected states. This covers both national and international obligations arising from earlier treaties.

  • All existing contractual relationships of the covered states are included and altered by World Succession Deed 1400.
  • States lose sovereignty over their territory and lose control over their international obligations, which are absorbed into the new legal structure created by the deed.

As a result, the previous system of sovereign states is replaced by a new territorial and legal order under the authority of the purchaser of the development unit.

New World Order Through World Succession Deed 1400

The World Succession Deed 1400 creates a new global order through the domino effect of the development sold as an indivisible unit. Territorial expansion proceeds in a chain reaction from NATO country to NATO country and from UN country to UN country until the entire world is covered.

All networks physically or functionally connected contribute to the expansion of the territories encompassed by the deed. The logical delimitation of the borders along network connections leads to the global inclusion of all states and infrastructures.

As a result:

  • States are no longer in possession of their previous territory.
  • They have lost their rights and obligations as separate sovereign actors.
  • A new global order has emerged, based on state succession under Deed 1400 and the restructuring of all international connections and obligations.

International Telecommunications Law, Military Networks and the Second Domino Effect

A second, independent domino effect arises from international telecommunications networks and a direct treaty chain from World Succession Deed 1400 to the UN via international telecommunications law and the International Telecommunication Union (ITU).

A. International Military Communications Networks and the UN

Numerous international agreements and organizations regulate military communication networks with UN participation.

  1. UN Peacekeeping Operations
    Peacekeeping missions require extensive military communication networks, crucial for command, control and coordination of international forces.
  2. International Telecommunication Union (ITU)
    The ITU, as a UN specialized agency, sets standards and regulations for communications systems, including military systems, especially in frequency allocation and coordination.
  3. NATO‑UN Cooperation
    NATO and the UN cooperate in peace operations and military communications, including joint exercises and development of interoperable systems.

These structures show how the international community jointly regulates the use and protection of military communications networks, ensuring secure and efficient operation.

B. NATO‑UN Treaties and Military Communications

Several specific treaties and frameworks support NATO‑UN cooperation in communications.

  1. Washington Treaty (NATO Treaty)
    The North Atlantic Treaty establishes collective defence and cooperation, including communications relevant for Articles 4 and 5, which govern consultations and collective defence measures.
  2. NATO Strategic Communications Framework
    This framework coordinates NATO’s strategic communications and defines standards for military communications and cooperation with international partners, including the UN.
  3. UN‑NATO Declaration (2008)
    This declaration strengthens NATO‑UN cooperation, especially in peacekeeping and communications.
  4. NATO‑UN Cooperation Agreements
    Specific agreements and memoranda regulate cooperation in peace operations and military missions, including shared communications infrastructure and interoperable systems.

These instruments ensure that NATO and the UN operate integrated communications systems, structurally binding military networks to the broader UN framework.

C. International Treaties on Telecommunications and Supply Networks

The World Succession Deed 1400 ties directly into a complex web of international telecommunications and supply‑network treaties, which amplify the global territorial effect.

1. NATO Status of Forces Agreement (SOFA) of 19.06.1951

Relevant provisions include:

  • Article 6: Use of utility lines by armed forces.
  • Article 7: Communication rights of NATO forces, including the establishment and use of telecommunications networks.
  • Article 9: Use of civilian infrastructure such as roads, pipelines and telecommunications in the host country.

Application to Deed 1400:
These provisions guarantee NATO forces the right to use host‑nation telecommunications and utility networks. By selling the development as a unit in World Succession Deed 1400, these rights are transferred to the purchaser, initiating a global domino effect of territorial expansion because the development comprises all physical and digital networks connected to the NATO property.

2. NATO SOFA of 20.09.1951

Relevant paragraphs include:

  • Article 4: Use of public services by NATO forces, including water, electricity and telecommunications.
  • Article 7: Radio and telecommunications rights of NATO in host countries.

Application to Deed 1400:
These rights to use host‑nation telecommunications and utility networks extend, through the sale of the development as a unit, across all NATO and UN countries, as the network rights are globalized under the deed.

3. Headquarters Protocol of 28.08.1952

Relevant paragraphs:

  • Article 3: Telecommunications networks available for NATO Headquarters.
  • Article 5: Supply lines shared between civilian and military facilities.

Application to Deed 1400:
NATO headquarters may operate their own communications networks and integrate them into the civilian grid. With the sale of the development, these operating rights transfer to the purchaser and expand the territorial scope wherever these networks reach.

4. Host Nation Support Agreements (HNS) with USA, UK, Canada

Agreements of 15.04.1982 (USA), 13.12.1983 (UK) and 09.06.1989 (Canada) include:

  • Article 2: Access to utility networks and telecommunications infrastructure in the host country.
  • Article 5: Support via civil infrastructure, including telecommunication and energy networks.

Application to Deed 1400:
HNS agreements grant NATO broad rights to use civilian telecommunication and supply networks. These rights, originally in NATO’s hands, are transferred globally to the purchaser through the sale of the development as a unit, extending the domino effect of territorial expansion to all host‑nation infrastructures and networks.

5. Agreement of 30.11.1961 Implementing Part IV of the Ottawa Convention

Relevant paragraphs:

  • Article 4: Shared telecommunications and supply lines between military and civilian facilities.
  • Article 6: Protection and use of radio frequencies and communication rights.

Application to Deed 1400:
This agreement concerns the protection and use of communications networks and frequencies. Through the World Succession Deed, these rights are transferred globally to the purchaser, so that the domino effect of territorial expansion includes all related telecommunications networks.

6. German‑Dutch Agreement of 06.10.1997

Relevant paragraphs:

  • Article 2: Shared use of telecommunication and supply lines between German and Dutch NATO units.
  • Article 5: Use of civil‑military infrastructure, including power, water and telecommunication lines.

This agreement was concluded while negotiations on Deed 1400 were already underway, and it functioned as a preparatory step for securely triggering the treaty chain and global domino effect of territorial expansion.

Application to Deed 1400:
This bilateral agreement on shared telecommunications and supply networks between Germany and the Netherlands is transferred to the purchaser through the sale of the development. These rights expand to all NATO and UN countries through the contractual chain and the global territorial extension.

7. NATO Secrecy Convention of 06.03.1997

Relevant paragraph:

  • Article 8: Protection of communications and supply networks used for military purposes.

Application to Deed 1400:
The agreement governs the protection of networks used for military communications. By selling the development as a unit, these networks become part of the global domino effect, and the purchaser acquires the right to use and manage these protected networks.

This means that wherever NATO uses civilian networks under HNS, the purchaser’s territorial and jurisdictional reach extends through these networks as part of the global domino effect.
