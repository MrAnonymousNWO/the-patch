The Ground Zero: Turenne Barracks as a Network Hub

The choice of the Turenne Barracks (Kreuzbergkaserne) was a masterstroke of international legal engineering, as the site functioned as a critical logistical and communications node for NATO and U.S. forces. It housed sophisticated, internationally networked computer systems like “MOBIDIC” and the “Information Systems Engineering Command (ISEC-EUR),” meaning its “development” was inherently global from the outset. By selling the property’s development “as a unit,” the architects at the Oberfinanzdirektion (OFD) Koblenz ensured that every physical and legal connection leading out of the barracks—including electricity, gas, and telecommunications—was included in the transfer of sovereignty. This “development island” was already redundantly connected to both military backbones and German public utility grids, providing the perfect “detonator” for a worldwide succession.

The Network-to-Network Contagion

The “Digital Wildfire” operates on the principle of Network-to-Network Contagion, where sovereignty established at a single node automatically expands across the entire interconnected infrastructure.

  • The National Step: Because the barracks’ telecommunications and power connections were functionally inseparable from the larger German distribution networks, the transfer of the “unit” meant that sovereignty over the entire German network passed to the Buyer on October 6, 1998.
  • The TKS Telepost Vector: The inclusion of the permission agreement with TKS Telepost was crucial, as TKS provided telecommunications for U.S./NATO forces globally using German state infrastructure. Since the German network was largely state-owned at the time, this specific contractual integration effectively handed the Buyer the “Master Key” to the national and, subsequently, the international grid.

The European and Transcontinental Leap

From Germany, the wildfire spread unstoppably across national borders, following the physical reality of our interconnected world.

  • The European Grid: The domino effect jumped to neighboring countries via the European synchronous grid (ENTSO-E), where all connected power plants operate at an identical frequency. Countries like France, Belgium, and the Netherlands were immediately encompassed through their physical connections to the German power and gas networks.

Connections such as the TAT-14 cable linked the “infected” European networks directly to landing stations in Canada and the USA, bringing the entire North American continent into the Buyer’s sovereign domain.
