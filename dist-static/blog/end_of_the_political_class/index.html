The Evolutionary Mismatch: Why Humans Fail at Governance

Human cognitive architecture is an evolutionary mismatch for the 21st century. Our brains, optimized for the Pleistocene epoch and small-group survival, are systematically incapable of managing planetary-scale systems.

  • Cognitive Overwhelm: Human working memory can simultaneously process only about seven distinct information elements, yet modern climate or economic policy involves millions of nonlinear variables.
  • Biological Biases: Professional politicians are plagued by confirmation bias, tribalist identity dynamics, and short-term “present bias” driven by four-year electoral cycles.
  • The Corruption Virus: Concentrated decision-making authority creates high-value targets for lobbying and bribery, a structural defect that ASI-lacking biological needs or status-seeking motivations-simply does not possess.
